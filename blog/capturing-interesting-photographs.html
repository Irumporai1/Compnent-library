How to capture interesting photographs?

Published on Aug 15, 2021 · 2 min read

Photography is the art of capturing light with a camera, usually via a digital sensor or film, to create an image. In the digital era, with wide range of cameras from full-frame mirrorless camera to mobile phone cameras available to the end users, the challenge is to capture an interesting photo that could get the viewers attention.

Playing with Aperture, Shutter Speed,ISO

One way of creating best photos is understanding the basics. Aperture, Shutter Speed,ISO are the three controls which defines how a photograph is captured. Capturing still object, fast moving object, objects in dark, everything is captured using the combination of these three controls.

Shutter Speed

Moving objects should be captured with high shutter speed(Ex: 1/400). Relatively a slow shutter speed(Ex: 1/60 approx) can be used for still objects.

Aperture

An Aperture is the opening within a lens that controls how much light hits the imaging sensor.It also defines the focus range. Highest aperture (low values - 1.4) are used for portraits. Lowest aperture(high values - 14) are used for landscapes.

Combination of all the three

These three also indirectly contribute to the amount of light that enters the sensor when a photo is taken. So playing with different values may produce stunning photographs.

Conclusion

Also you can apply other techniques like reflections, symmetry, leading lines, patterns, point of view to create interesting frames.

There is no limit for your creativity. The more you observe and use your pro settings in the camera, you will be able to take better photos. Happy Clicking.
